Empowering Informal Traders: An urban renewal proposal for Nyanga

Summary

The City of Cape Town commissioned SUN Development to develop an urban regeneration plan for the Nyanga Public Transport Interchange. Taking a data-driven, systemic approach, we explored solutions to improve infrastructure, safety, and the management of informal trade. My role focused on conducting in-depth research on informal trade, mapping its dynamics, dependencies, and challenges. Using these insights, I developed design principles to ensure the broader regeneration framework was inclusive of traders, aligned with their daily realities, and supported economic opportunities.

Project background and brief

The Municipality's perspective: A congested and unregulated urban hub

Nyanga, one of Cape Town’s oldest historically segregated townships, is a densely populated area shaped by apartheid-era policies and ongoing socio-economic challenges.

The municipality identified a critical urban node for an upgrade, centered around a major public transport interchange and a dense cluster of informal traders. The area faced deeply interconnected issues, including urban congestion, crime, and limited infrastructure, all rooted in the legacy of top-down urban planning.

The brief: A regeneration proposal to address symptomatic issues

The City sought solutions to manage congestion, improve safety, and regulate informal trade. Their desired outcome was a proposal that included a trading plan, a business and maintenance strategy, and infrastructure upgrades to formalize and structure the area.

SUN Development was commissioned to develop this strategy, balancing policy, economic activity, and urban functionality.
