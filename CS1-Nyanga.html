From Insight to Inclusion: A Design Strategy for Informal Trade

Project background

In 2013, the Municipality of Cape Town identified a vibrant but unsafe and under-serviced public transport hub in Nyanga for regeneration. As part of an NPC contracted to deliver an insight-driven urban design proposal, I conducted research into the lived experiences of the local informal traders that were central to the area, surfacing actionable insights to shape a regeneration plan that was inclusive of their needs.

My role involved

Ethnographic user research

Stakeholder engagement

Systems thinking

Participatory design

Why was an upgrade commissioned?

Municipal challenges with public safety and informal trade in an under-resourced public transport hub, serving 7000 commuters per day

The project was based in Nyanga, Cape Town—a densely populated, low-income neighbourhood facing complex socio-economic challenges. The municipality planned to upgrade a critical public transport interchange due to ongoing issues with crime, congestion, unregulated informal trade, and informal taxi operations. Hundreds of traders sold goods in the area, filling key gaps in the local supply chain. However, from the City’s perspective, this activity contributed to sidewalk congestion and did not comply with permitting laws or environmental health standards. SUN Development was commissioned to design an upgrade that improved public safety and included a comprehensive trading plan.

My contribution to the multi-faceted design proposal

I researched the workings of informal traders and translated the insights into design guidelines that informed the final trading plan.

As a researcher and architect on the team my role included:

  • Qualitative and quantitative field research
  • Collaboration with government and community stakeholders, as well as multidisciplinary professionals
  • Communicating insights through maps, infographics and reports
  • Creating design principles and collaborating on their iterative application to a final design
  • Facilitating participatory design sessions with stakeholders

The objective

The objective was to ensure the final trading plan would be inclusive of traders and responsive to their reality. This required a deep understanding of how informal trading functioned, the needs of traders, and the potential positive or negative impacts a proposed upgrade could have on them.

The strategy

Insight-driven design principles, validated and refined through a participatory process.
