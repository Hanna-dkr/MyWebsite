Empowering Informal Traders: An urban renewal proposal for Nyanga

Summary

The City of Cape Town commissioned SUN Development to develop an urban regeneration plan for the Nyanga Public Transport Interchange. Taking a data-driven, systemic approach, we explored solutions to improve infrastructure, safety, and the management of informal trade. My role focused on conducting in-depth research on informal trade, mapping its dynamics, dependencies, and challenges. Using these insights, I developed design principles to ensure the broader regeneration framework was inclusive of traders, aligned with their daily realities, and supported economic opportunities.

My role involved

Ethnographic user research

Stakeholder engagement

Systems thinking & service design

Community co-design

Project background:

The local municipality needed support in planning the upgrade of a complex area in Nyanga

Nyanga is one of South Africa's oldest 'townships' - it is a densely populated, historically segregated urban area, marked by informal housing, limited infrastructure, and vibrant community life, shaped by apartheid-era policies and ongoing socio-economic challenges.

The municipality had identified the area around a primary public transport interchange for an upgrade — this was a busy and complex node of activity, combining buses, taxis and a dense network of informal traders.

The brief

Our team was tasked with addressing three problem areas through an urban regeneration proposal

Nyanga faced deeply interconnected challenges rooted in the legacy of top-down, apartheid planning.

The City had identified surface-level symptoms—congestion, crime, and unregulated trade —and expected that SUN Development would be addressed through some form of trading plan, business and maintenance strategy, and infrastructure upgrades.
